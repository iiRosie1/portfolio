Banana Split

UX/UI Design User Research Figma

Working in a group of four, for our GBDA 210: Introduction to User Experience course, we created Banana Split. A collaborative grocery shopping app designed to help university students save money and reduce food waste by sharing bulk purchases.

Problem Statement

Grocery shopping is an essential part of university student life, and can pose problems when it comes to managing financial constraints. University students have noticed that groceries have become more expensive, and having that on top of tuition and housing expenses affects their nutrition. According to CBC News, food prices in 2025 could increase by 5% (Heydari 2024). Existing research shows that food costs are on the rise, and in consequence affordability becomes an important factor when it comes to grocery shopping.

According to Investopedia, buying in bulk does decrease the cost per unit, but often leads to overconsumption and waste. As a result, students miss out on savings.

Our app addresses these challenges by providing a platform where users can find grocery deals, easily find others interested in splitting bulk groceries if needed, coordinate purchases, and handle payments seamlessly. By facilitating collaborative shopping, the app promotes financial savings, sustainability, and good grocery shopping habits.

User Research

We interviewed various university students (ages 18–21) who regularly cook their own meals. The interviews focused on current grocery habits, pain points, and existing workarounds.

Store Preferences

Students shop at stores like Costco, T&T, and No Frills for affordability, selection, and proximity.

Budget Awareness

All participants actively budget and look for deals using apps like Flipp—though flyer apps often miss products they need.

Transportation Barriers

Public transit limits how much they can carry, influencing how often and where they shop.

Desire for Tools

Students want smarter, social tools to coordinate trips, split costs, and compare prices before going out.

Reflection

Working on Banana Split showed us that affordability alone isn't enough. A good solution needs to fit within students' routines and social lives.

What We Learned

  • Users value simplicity, trust, and consistency, don't try to overload a bunch of features in your app. Focus on one core feature and design the app around that.
  • User testing is so so so valuable in gaining insight and ideas you may of missed
